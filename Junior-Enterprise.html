A junior enterprise is a civil social nonprofit organization established and executed entirely by students of a university or a business school. The purpose of the organization is to provide services for companies, institutions and society, often in the form of consultancies, while enriching the learning of its members with practical experience in the field of their studies. 

Often, a junior enterprise is linked to a particular engineering university or business school.The most common expertise areas for junior enterprises are business and management, engineering, marketing, communication, IT services and law. 

The members of the organization have the chance to take part in real-world projects, while experiencing the functioning of a real company: junior enterprises, indeed, either are real companies, or resemble one in their operational activities, often having a management council and an executive board, together with an own regulation.
